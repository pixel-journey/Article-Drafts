# $KING of Pixals: The Historic Meme That Graduated

**The First (and Thus Far Only) Meme to Successfully Bond and Graduate on WAX**

In December 2024, Pixel Journey launched **$KING of Pixals** on the then-nascent wax.fun platform. ## 1. Origin Story: The First Bonded Meme on WAX

$KING launched as one of the very first memes on the wax.fun bonding curve. It was a pure fair-launch experiment with no pre-allocation hype or paid promotion — just genuine community backing from the Pixel Journey fam.

Key early milestones:

## 2. Graduation to Alcor & Real Liquidity

On January 7, 2025, $KING successfully graduated from wax.fun and bonded to **Alcor Exchange** at approximately $44k market cap with 170k WAXP in initial liquidity.

This was a historic moment — the first meme on WAX to make this transition successfully. The distribution was textbook fair-launch:
- 20% remained in the bonding curve
- 13.37% allocated to pixeljourney
- 6.9% to the creator (piga.pxj)
- The rest widely dispersed among 192 initial holders

Over 30% of total supply was immediately burned into LP positions, including a major 10% KING + 60M PXJ pair established by the project itself.

## 3. Deflationary Mechanics & Fee Burning

What makes $KING special is its built-in deflationary engine:
- The dedicated KING/PXJ liquidity pool on Alcor (plus additional pairs involving WPIXAL) captures volume fees from trades across the broader ecosystem.
- These fees are **permanently burned**, removing both KING and PXJ from circulation with every transaction.
- In its first full year, the mechanism has already burned several percent of total KING supply and a meaningful portion of circulating PXJ.
- It has also accumulated more than 5,500 WAXP in fees.

This makes $KING one of the most effective native deflationary tools on WAX — turning trading activity into permanent scarcity for both itself and PXJ.
