# Security, Scams & Best Practices in the Pixel Journey

**Protecting Yourself While Exploring the Adventure**

As with any Web3 project, staying safe is essential. The Pixel Journey has a strong, helpful community and official channels, but scammers are always active. This guide covers the most important security practices, common scams to watch for, and how to participate confidently and securely.

This is the definitive practical guide to security and best practices in the Pixel Journey.

## 1. Core Security Principles

- Never share your seed phrase or private keys with anyone
- Always double-check URLs and use official links only
- Start small when trying new features or bridges
- Verify information through official Discord channels and docs
- Be skeptical of unsolicited DMs, "support" accounts, or too-good-to-be-true offers
- Use hardware wallets or strong security practices for larger holdings

## 2. Common Scams Targeting Pixel Journey Participants

### Fake Support / DM Scams
Scammers often impersonate team members or "support" in DMs on Discord, X, or Telegram. They may claim there’s an issue with your wallet, staking, or airdrop and ask for your seed phrase or to connect your wallet to a fake site.

**Rule:** The official team and moderators will **never** ask for your seed phrase or ask you to connect your wallet via DM.

### Fake Websites & Phishing
Fake versions of stake.pixeljourney.xyz, NFTHive, AtomicHub, or bridge sites are common. Always verify the URL and bookmark official links.

### Fake Airdrops & Giveaways
Scammers create fake airdrop campaigns or "claim" sites that require connecting your wallet or approving malicious transactions.

### Impersonator Accounts
Fake X/Twitter, Discord, or Telegram accounts pretending to be official Pixel Journey accounts or team members.

### Rug Pull / Fake Project Scams
Scammers sometimes create copycat projects or tokens claiming to be related to Pixel Journey.

## 3. How to Verify Official Information

- Official website & docs: docs.pixeljourney.xyz
- Official staking portal: stake.pixeljourney.xyz
- Official Discord: Check pinned messages and verified roles
- Official X account: @PxJourney (verify the handle)
- Never trust links sent in DMs
- Cross-check important announcements in multiple official channels

## 4. Safe Participation Habits

- Bookmark official sites and never click links from messages
- Use a dedicated wallet for Pixel Journey activities (separate from high-value wallets if possible)
- Enable transaction simulation / preview features when available
- Regularly review connected contracts and revoke approvals if needed
- Keep software and wallets updated
- Be extra cautious when bridging or interacting with new contracts
- Participate in governance and community discussions to stay informed

## 5. What to Do If Something Seems Wrong

- Do not click suspicious links or approve transactions
- Ask in official Discord channels (never DM random people)
- Report suspicious accounts to moderators
- If you approved a malicious contract, revoke approvals immediately using tools like revoke.cash or similar
- Document everything (screenshots, transaction hashes) if you believe you’ve been targeted

## 6. Building Good Habits Long-Term

Security is not a one-time thing. As the ecosystem grows with new Px-dApps, bridges, and features, new attack vectors may appear. Staying cautious, verifying information, and participating through official channels will keep you safe while enjoying everything the Pixel Journey has to offer.

The community is generally very helpful with security questions — don’t hesitate to ask in the right channels.

## Conclusion: Stay Safe, Stay in the Journey

The Pixel Journey is meant to be explored and enjoyed. By following basic security practices and staying vigilant, you can participate confidently for years to come. The community and team have built something worth protecting — and that includes protecting yourself while being part of it.

**Stay sharp. Stay safe. The pixels are worth it.**
